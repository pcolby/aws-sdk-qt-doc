60  * @brief Client for AWS Budgets
61  *
62  * Budgets enable you to plan your service usage, service costs, and your RI utilization. You can also track how close your
63  * plan is to your budgeted amount or to the free tier limits. Budgets provide you with a quick way to see your
64  * usage-to-date and current estimated charges from AWS and to see how much your predicted usage accrues in charges by the
65  * end of the month. Budgets also compare current estimates and charges to the amount that you indicated you want to use or
66  * spend and lets you see how much of your budget has been used. AWS updates your budget status several times a day.
67  * Budgets track your unblended costs, subscriptions, and refunds. You can create the following types of
68  *
69  * budgets> <ul> <li>
70  *
71  * Cost budgets allow you to say how much you want to spend on a
72  *
73  * service> </li> <li>
74  *
75  * Usage budgets allow you to say how many hours you want to use for one or more
76  *
77  * services> </li> <li>
78  *
79  * RI utilization budgets allow you to define a utilization threshold and receive alerts when RIs are tracking below that
80  *
81  * threshold> </li> </ul>
82  *
83  * You can create up to 20,000 budgets per AWS master account. Your first two budgets are free of charge. Each additional
84  * budget costs $0.02 per day. You can set up optional notifications that warn you if you exceed, or are forecasted to
85  * exceed, your budgeted amount. You can have notifications sent to an Amazon SNS topic, to an email address, or to both.
86  * For more information, see <a
87  * href="https://docs.aws.amazon.com/awsaccountbilling/latest/aboutv2/budgets-sns-policy.html">Creating an Amazon SNS Topic
88  * for Budget Notifications</a>. AWS Free Tier usage alerts via AWS Budgets are provided for you, and do not count toward
89  * your budget

91  * limits>
